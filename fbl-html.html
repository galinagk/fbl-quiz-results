Your overall score tells you how you're doing compared to the rest of the folks who have taken the quiz. To get this score, your individual step scores are weighted by the relative difficulty of each step, and then added together.

What do these results mean?

Each bar refers to one step in the complete feedback loop. Your score is denoted by the blue bar. But this isn't just a simple strong and weak scale because the steps of the feedback loop are not equally easy. The red line in each step represents the "benchmark," the average score of other organizations that have taken this Quiz, so you can see how your organization compares. As you see here, for example, dialogue (with a community) and course correct (making decisions based on the feedback and evidence from a community) are much harder than collecting and analyzing.
